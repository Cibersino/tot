Google OCR Privacy

Google OCR applies only when you choose that OCR route inside the app.

  • Authorization: the app launches the system browser so you can authorize Google access yourself.
  • Files sent to Google: only the files you explicitly choose for OCR are sent to Google for that OCR operation.
  • Temporary Google-side artifact: after text export, the app attempts to delete the temporary converted Google document created for the OCR flow.
  • Google account used for OCR: the OCR route runs under the end user's Google account.
  • Google Drive permission requested: the app requests the minimum practical Drive permission, drive.file.

toT uses an owner-provided Google Cloud project and OAuth client distributed with the app. Ordinary users do not need to manually obtain or import OAuth client credentials.

The app stores local OCR connection/token state on the device so the OCR connection can be reused. If you disconnect Google OCR inside the app, the app is expected to revoke the saved token and remove the local OCR token state. You can also revoke the app's Google access from your Google Account permissions/security controls.

The OCR files you select for processing are not sent to the developers as part of the OCR flow. Google may process those selected files under Google's own services and policies in order to provide the OCR operation.
